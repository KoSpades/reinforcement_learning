## 04/25/26

We are starting out this project to see if there's any opportunity to find "grounded signals" from NL text, taking inspirations from Silver and Sutton's "Welcome to the age of experience".

We will explore this to see if there's any opportunities for a one-month project. 

What we are looking for: 
1. the settings should be similar to settings where RL tend to perform well (else it makes little sense).
2. the setting should also be currently underexplored (else people would be using RL directly, such as finance), but NL gives opportunities to find such signals. Note that this is in conflict with 1, to some degree.
3. the setting should be actionable: people take an action, and observe some rewards. 

Our question:"Are these setting abundant? Or are we really approaching the end of the era of human data?" We will try to produce an answer to this question.

Potential framing of the problem "Much of the discussion around the “era of experience” suggests that future learning systems must move beyond static human data toward interactive experience. We ask whether some human text already contains traces of experience: records of states, actions, and grounded reward signals produced by real-world decision-makers. We study where such grounded decision traces appear, when natural language adds value beyond structured data, and whether these traces could form the basis for future learning-from-experience systems."

Read the 2017 original paper on RLHF. It's mostly about aligning with human preference, using human preference. It's less related to our goal: "finding grounded signals", but if later we find that we can pivot to "finding human preference from NL", we can do that.

We should keep our focus sharp: our objective is just to "collect grounded rewards".
